Teen Dietary Balance Project

Welcome to our team project! Our goal is to raise awareness among teenagers about healthy eating habits across different cultures.

Why a Balanced Diet Matters

A balanced diet keeps us healthy and energized. It helps our body get the right mix of nutrients for daily activities, growth, and mental sharpness.

For teens, kids, and expecting mothers, proper nutrition is essential for development and reproduction. Key nutrients like calcium, iron, and folic acid are vital.

Eating well boosts energy, lowers disease risks, and regulates essential functions like blood sugar and pressure.

General Tips:

  • Drink at least 1.5–2 liters of water daily
  • Include protein in every meal
  • Avoid sugary foods, sodas, and ultra-processed snacks
  • Adjust portions based on physical activity

Current Trends in Italy

A recent survey of 89,321 teens revealed:

  • Only 7.5% exercise daily, 13.8% never do
  • 26.5% of 17-year-olds spend over 5 hours/day on social media
  • 26.8% skip breakfast, 12% eat sweets daily
  • Low fruit/veg intake: 6% never eat fruit, 7.4% never eat vegetables
  • Excess fats and sugars: consumed 2–4 times a week by 29.4%, and daily by 26.2%

A Global Perspective

WHO/Europe highlights:

  • Less than 40% of teens eat fruits or veggies daily
  • 25% consume sweets every day
  • 15% drink sugary drinks daily, mostly among lower-income families
  • Physical inactivity is widespread
